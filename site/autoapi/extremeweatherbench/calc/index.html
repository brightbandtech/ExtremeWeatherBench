    dewpoint_from_specific_humidity 

    dewpoint_from_specific_humidity(
    pressure, specific_humidity
)

    Calculate dewpoint from specific humidity.

    This computation follows the methodology used in metpy.calc.dewpoint_from_specific_humidity. Given specific humidity $q$, mixing ratio $w$, and pressure $p$, we compute first $w = q / (1 - q)$ and $e = p w / (w + \epsilon)$ where $\epsilon=0.622$ is the ratio of the molecular weights of water and dry air and $e$ is the partial pressure of water vapor.

    Then, we invert the Bolton (1980) formula to get the dewpoint $T_d$ given $e$:

    $$T_d =\frac{243.5 \log(e / 6.112)}{17.67 - \log(e / 6.112)}$$

    Parameters:

    • pressure (float) –

      The ambient pressure in hPa.

    • specific_humidity (float) –

      The specific humidity in kg/kg.

    Returns:

    • float –

      The dewpoint in Kelvin.

    find_next_landfall_for_init_time 

    find_next_landfall_for_init_time(
    forecast_landfalls, target_landfalls
)

    Find unique next upcoming landfalls from target data.

    For each forecast initialization time, finds the next landfall event in the target data that occurs after that init_time. Only unique landfalls are returned (multiple init_times mapping to the same landfall are deduplicated). Init_times without future landfalls are excluded.

    Parameters:

    • forecast_landfalls (DataArray) –

      Forecast landfall DataArray with init_time dimension (from find_landfalls)

    • target_landfalls (DataArray) –

      Target landfall DataArray from find_landfalls with return_all=True (has landfall dimension)

    Returns:

    • Optional[DataArray] –

      DataArray with unique next landfalls indexed by init_time, or

    • Optional[DataArray] –

      None if no future landfalls exist

    mixing_ratio 

    mixing_ratio(partial_pressure, total_pressure)

    Calculate the mixing ratio of water vapor in air.

    Uses the formula: $w = (\epsilon * e) / (p - e)$ where $\epsilon = 0.622$.

    Parameters:

    • partial_pressure (float | NDArray) –

      Water vapor partial pressure in hPa.

    • total_pressure (float | NDArray) –

      Total atmospheric pressure in hPa.

    Returns:

    • float | NDArray[float64] –

      numpy.ndarray: Mixing ratio in kg/kg (dimensionless).

    Notes
    • Mixing ratio is approximately constant with height for unsaturated air
    • Values typically range from 0 to ~0.025 kg/kg in the atmosphere
    • ε (epsilon) = 0.622 is the ratio of molecular weights (H2O/dry air)

    saturation_vapor_pressure 

    saturation_vapor_pressure(temperature)

    Calculate saturation vapor pressure using the Clausius-Clapeyron equation.

    Uses the Magnus formula approximation which is accurate for temperatures between -40°C and +50°C. Formula:

    $$e_ = 6.112 \times \exp\left(\frac{17.67*T}{T+243.5}\right)$$

    Parameters:

    • temperature (float | NDArray) –

      Temperature values in Celsius (can be scalar or array).

    Returns:

    • NDArray[float64] –

      numpy.ndarray: Saturation vapor pressure values in hPa.

    Notes
    • Based on the Magnus formula which is accurate within ±0.1% for typical atmospheric temperatures
    • Saturation vapor pressure increases exponentially with temperature
    • At 0°C: ~6.11 hPa, at 20°C: ~23.4 hPa, at 30°C: ~42.4 hPa
